🔍 What This Result Means

The Kedarnath disaster on June 16-17, 2013 was triggered by an extreme cloudburst — a sudden, highly localised intense rainfall event. Our system analyses NASA POWER satellite data at a 50×70 km grid resolution.

The pre-disaster risk score of 3.00/4.0 in the Kedarnath grid cell reflects what the satellite data showed in the days leading up to the event — humidity levels, soil saturation, and accumulated rainfall patterns.

Key limitation: Cloudbursts are extremely localised. NASA POWER data averages conditions across a 50×70 km cell. The actual cloudburst may have been far more intense in the Kedarnath valley specifically. Incorporating IMD rain gauge station data would significantly improve detection of such events.
